# Tracking Your SDI Claim

Knowing how to track your claim helps you catch issues early and plan financially.

## Online Tracking

### Setting Up Online Access

1. Go to [myedd.edd.ca.gov](https://myedd.edd.ca.gov)
2. Log in with your account credentials
3. Navigate to "Disability Insurance"
4. View your claim dashboard

::callout{type="tip" title="Check Regularly"}
Check your claim status every 2-3 days during active processing. EDD sometimes updates status without sending notifications.
::

### What You'll See Online

**Claim Information:**
- Claim ID
- Disability begin date
- Current status
- Payment history

**Messages/Correspondence:**
- Any requests from EDD
- Determination letters
- Important notices

**Payment History:**
- Dates of payments
- Amounts paid
- Payment method used

## Status Codes Explained

### Common Status Meanings

| Status | Meaning | Action Needed |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Received | Claim in queue | None - wait |
| Pending | Under review | None - wait |
| Incomplete | Missing info | Check mail/messages |
| Medical Review | Doctor verification | Verify doctor received requests |
| Approved | Claim approved | Payment coming soon |
| Paid | Payment issued | Check bank/card |
| Denied | Claim rejected | Review letter, consider appeal |
| Suspended | On hold | Contact EDD immediately |

### Status That Need Attention

**Incomplete:**
Check for:
- Missing information in your application
- Requested documents
- Online messages from EDD

**Suspended:**
Usually means:
- Potential fraud flag
- Missing continuing certification
- Issue requiring human review

## Phone Tracking

### When to Call

- Status hasn't updated in 3+ weeks
- Online status unclear
- Need information not available online
- Received confusing correspondence

### How to Call EDD

**Main SDI Line:**
1-800-480-3287

**Tips for Calling:**
- Call early morning (right when lines open)
- Tuesdays-Thursdays often have shorter waits
- Have SSN and claim ID ready
- Be prepared for 30-60+ minute wait
- Write down the name of who you speak with

### What to Ask

When you reach a representative:
1. Confirm your claim was received
2. Ask current status
3. Ask if anything is needed from you
4. Ask expected timeline
5. Get direct callback number if available

## Tracking Checklist

### Week 1 After Filing

- [ ] Confirmation number saved
- [ ] Online account access verified
- [ ] Check status once (should show "Received")

### Week 2 After Filing

- [ ] Check status online
- [ ] Confirm doctor was contacted
- [ ] Check mail for any correspondence

### Week 3 After Filing

- [ ] Check status online
- [ ] If no update, call EDD
- [ ] Review any messages in online portal

### Week 4+ After Filing

- [ ] Continue checking every 2-3 days
- [ ] If approved, watch for payment
- [ ] Begin continuing certifications if required

## Common Tracking Issues

### Status Never Changes

**Possible Causes:**
- Claim stuck in queue (high volume)
- Missing information
- Issue with doctor verification

**Solutions:**
1. Check online messages thoroughly
2. Call EDD for status update
3. Visit in person if phone is unhelpful

### Can't Access Online Account

**Possible Causes:**
- Incorrect login credentials
- Account locked
- System issues

**Solutions:**
1. Reset password
2. Try different browser
3. Call EDD tech support
4. Use phone to check status instead

### Conflicting Information

**Possible Causes:**
- System delays in updating
- Human error
- Different parts of claim updating separately

**Solutions:**
1. Wait 24-48 hours and recheck
2. Call EDD for clarification
3. Ask representative to note your file

## Proactive Tracking Tips

### Stay Ahead of Problems

1. **Calendar reminders:** Set for key dates
2. **Document everything:** Keep a log of status checks
3. **Save screenshots:** Of online status pages
4. **Keep call records:** Date, time, who you spoke with

### Tracking Log Template

Keep a simple log:

```
Date        | Action          | Status/Result
------------|-----------------|------------------
03/15/2026  | Filed claim     | Confirmation #12345
03/18/2026  | Checked online  | Status: Received
03/22/2026  | Checked online  | Status: Pending Medical
03/25/2026  | Called doctor   | They'll certify tomorrow
03/29/2026  | Checked online  | Status: Approved
```

## When to Escalate

### Signs Escalation Is Needed

- 4+ weeks with no progress
- EDD unresponsive to calls
- Conflicting information received
- Urgent financial need

### Escalation Options

1. **Visit in person** - Talk to someone directly
2. **Contact Assembly member** - They have EDD liaisons
3. **Contact ALPA** - Union may be able to assist
4. **Document for formal complaint** - If needed later
